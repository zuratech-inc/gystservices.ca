In a world that moves fast and asks us to keep up, there is profound wisdom in returning to the simple truths taught by our elders. These teachings are not relics of the past but living frameworks for navigating the complexity of modern life.

## The Wisdom of Simplicity

Growing up in the North, I learned early that the most important truths are often the simplest ones. In small, isolated communities, you learn quickly what matters: connection, respect, honesty, and care for one another. These values were not taught in classrooms but lived in everyday interactions.

> When life gets complicated, as it often does, I have learned to simplify by returning to core values.

## Physical, Mental, Emotional, Spiritual

Indigenous teachings offer a holistic framework for understanding ourselves. Rather than separating mind from body, thought from feeling, we are invited to see ourselves as integrated beings with four interconnected dimensions: physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual.

Each dimension needs daily attention. When we neglect one, the others feel the strain. A physical ailment affects our mood. Emotional turmoil clouds our thinking. Spiritual disconnection leaves us feeling lost even when everything else seems fine on the surface.

## When Life Gets Complicated

Modern life has a way of pulling us in countless directions. Work demands, family obligations, social pressures, financial worries, and the constant noise of digital life can leave us feeling fragmented and overwhelmed.

In these moments, the instinct is often to do more, try harder, and push through. But there is another way.

## Returning to What Matters

The practice of returning to core values is not about escaping complexity but about finding clarity within it. When we know what we stand on, we can navigate uncertainty with greater confidence and less anxiety.

This is true whether you are Indigenous, a newcomer finding your way, or anyone navigating a life transition. The specifics of our cultural teachings may differ, but the principle is universal: ground yourself in what matters most, and the path forward becomes clearer.

## Practical Steps Forward

- Identify your three to five core values and write them down
- Create a daily check-in practice across physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual dimensions
- When feeling overwhelmed, pause and ask: what would my values guide me to do right now?
- Share these practices with someone you trust
- Consider counselling as a space to explore and strengthen your connection to your values
