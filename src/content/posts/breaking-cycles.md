We carry more from our families than we realize. The way we handle conflict, express love, manage stress, and relate to others is deeply shaped by patterns that may stretch back generations.

## What Are Intergenerational Patterns

Family Systems Therapy teaches us that we are best understood not as isolated individuals but as members of interconnected systems. Within these systems, patterns of behaviour, belief, and emotional response are passed from one generation to the next.

These patterns are not always harmful. Many families pass down beautiful traditions of resilience, generosity, and connection. But some patterns cause pain, and they often operate below our conscious awareness.

## How Patterns Travel

Intergenerational patterns travel through both explicit teaching and implicit modelling. We learn how to be in the world by watching the people closest to us. A child who grows up in a home where emotions are never discussed learns that feelings are not safe to express. This lesson carries forward.

> The patterns we do not examine are the patterns we repeat.

## Recognizing Your Patterns

The first step toward change is awareness. Consider these questions:

- What were the unspoken rules in your family growing up?
- How was conflict handled?
- How was love expressed?
- What roles did different family members play?
- Which of these patterns do you notice in your own life today?

## Breaking the Cycle

Breaking intergenerational cycles is not about blaming previous generations. It is about understanding, with compassion, how certain patterns developed and making conscious choices about what to carry forward.

This work takes courage. It means sitting with uncomfortable truths and choosing differently, even when the old way feels more familiar. Counselling provides a supported space for this exploration, helping you understand your family system and make intentional choices about the patterns you pass on.
