About Valerie

Valerie M. Sipos, M.C., CCC

Born in The Pas, Manitoba and raised in Canada's North, the Indigenous worldview is something I hold close and continues to guide me in everything I do. Third in a line of nine children, I learned at a very young age the value of simple basic truths.

I define myself physically, mentally, emotionally, and spiritually — and attend to each of these parts of myself daily. When life gets complicated, I have learned to simplify by returning to core values.

With years at Mount Royal University coaching Indigenous students and eight years at Hull Services supporting families healing from intergenerational trauma, I bring deep experience in culturally responsive care to my private practice. I'm a proud grandmother to six beautiful grandchildren who are the loves of my life.

Counselling Philosophy

A journey that requires courage

Counselling is a purposeful process that facilitates growth, direction, and wellness — it is all about change. A genuine interest in exploring what troubles you is the first brave step.

Collaborative

Therapeutic change is the collaborative work done by counsellor and client — identifying goals as well as a plan to realize them together.

Safe & Confidential

A purposefully safe and confidential place where we can explore the possibilities in change without judgment.

Guided by You

My intention is to be guided by your needs — meeting you where you are with genuine empathy and positive regard for your person.

"I would be honoured by your willingness to entrust me as a partner in this work."

— Valerie M. Sipos

Therapeutic Perspective

Grounded in culture, guided by theory

My therapeutic perspective is influenced at its foundation by cultural teachings and integrates three evidence-based frameworks.

01

Existentialism

A philosophical approach at whose core is the belief that each of us has the ability to find meaning in our lives. The counsellor works to understand you on your own subjective terms, encouraging reflection and taking responsibility for change.

02

Solutions-Focused Brief Therapy

Concentrating on small, realistic, and sustainable goals that result in positive outcomes. You are empowered as the expert in who you are — we focus on solutions and results rather than dwelling on problems.

03

Family Systems Therapy

Focusing on human problems from a relational perspective — we are best understood by how we interact within our systems. This explores intergenerational meanings, rules, processes, and cultural nuances.
